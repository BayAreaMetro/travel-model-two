work_df <- trips_df %>%
  filter(primary_purpose == "work") %>%
  group_by(destination) %>%
  summarise(work_trips = n(), .groups = "drop") %>%
  left_join(select(maz_df, mgra, emp_total), ., by = c("mgra" = "destination")) %>%
  filter(!is.na(work_trips))

ggplot(work_df, aes(x = emp_total, y = work_trips)) + geom_point()

zero_emp_work_df <- work_df %>%
  filter(emp_total < 1) %>%
  filter(work_trips > 0)

zero_work_emp_df <- work_df %>%
  filter(emp_total > 20) %>%
  filter(work_trips < 1)

Findings

The sampling appears to be implemented correctly. The correlation between work trip ends and total employment is 0.802671 at the MGRA level. The number of MGRAs with zero total employment and non-zero work trips is 829 (out of 24321). The number of MGRAs with total employment of at least 20 and zero work trips is 0.

For shopping trips, the correlation between shopping trip ends and retail employment is 0.8781834 at the MGRA level. The number of MGRAs with zero retail employment and non-zero shopping trips is 17153 (out of 24321), presumably because other size terms impact shopping trips? Note to SANDAG to check the size terms for shopping trips (why would it be something other than retail?). The number of MGRAs with retail employment of at least 20 and zero shopping trips is 0.

This evidence suggests the sampling is done correctly.
